## Where Experts Disagree

### 1. AI Content Usage

- **Author A (Neil Patel):**  
  Recommends using AI to speed up content creation, but with human editing for SEO optimization  
  (source: Neil Patel, https://neilpatel.com/blog/ai-seo, 01.2024)

- **Author B (Julian Goldie):**  
  Supports heavy AI automation to scale SEO content production quickly  
  (source: Julian Goldie, https://www.youtube.com/@JulianGoldieSEO, 2024)

- **My Decision:**  
  I choose a balanced approach — use AI for drafting but rely on human editing for quality and trust.  
  This avoids low-quality content while still benefiting from AI speed.

---

### 2. Backlinks vs Content Quality

- **Author A (Brian Dean):**  
  Emphasizes backlinks as a major ranking factor for SEO success  
  (source: Brian Dean, https://backlinko.com/hub/seo, 03.2023)

- **Author B (Ahrefs):**  
  Emphasizes search intent and content quality over backlinks  
  (source: Ahrefs, https://ahrefs.com/blog/search-intent, 09.2023)

- **My Decision:**  
  I prioritize content quality first, then build backlinks.  
  Without strong content, backlinks alone won’t sustain rankings.

---

### 3. SEO Automation vs Manual Strategy

- **Author A (Matt Diggity):**  
  Supports testing and manual optimization to find what works best  
  (source: Matt Diggity, https://diggitymarketing.com/seo-strategy, 06.2023)

- **Author B (Julian Goldie):**  
  Promotes automation tools to scale SEO processes quickly  
  (source: Julian Goldie, https://www.youtube.com/@JulianGoldieSEO, 2024)

- **My Decision:**  
  I prefer combining both — automate repetitive tasks but manually test and refine strategies.  
  This ensures scalability without losing accuracy.

---

### 4. Content Length vs Search Intent

- **Author A (Brian Dean):**  
  Suggests long-form content performs better in rankings  

- **Author B (Google Search Guidelines):**  
  States content should satisfy user intent, not focus on length  

- **My Decision:**  
  I follow search intent over length.  
  Writing only what is necessary improves user experience and avoids unnecessary content.
